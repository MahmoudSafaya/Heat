High body temperature. Keep your body cool Wear loose clothing. Electric fans are useful if inside temperatures are below 35 degrees. Keep your skin wet with a spray bottle or a sponge. Drink

Keep drinking water. Do not wait till you are thirsty.

Avoid caffeinated drinks and alcohol.

If you are going out in the heat, drink oral rehydration solutions.

Medicines

Keep your medicines in a cool place, away from direct sunlight.

Some medicines may be dangerous in hot weather, discuss them with your doctor.

Activity

Avoid strenuous activity.

Avoid using the oven or the hob.

Reschedule exercise or physiotherapy to cooler times.

Leaving Home

Avoid leaving the house during the hottest part of the day.

Prepare by doing your shopping before hot weather spells.

Take a water bottle with you.

Wear a hat and stay in the shade.

Use sunscreen SPF 15 or above.

Are you getting over-heated?

Be aware of symptoms of overheating as it may lead to dangerous complications.

Feeling thirsty.

Dry hot skin.

Feeling dizzy or uncoordinated.

Feeling sick.

Having cramps.

Swelling in your limbs.

High body temperature.

Wear loose clothing.

Electric fans are useful if inside temperatures are below 35°C.

Keep your skin wet with a spray bottle or a sponge.

Wrap a loose wet shawl or towel around your head.

Place a cold pack on your neck, armpits or groin.

Place your feet in a cool water basin.
Do not forget to dry your feet.

Use curtains to block direct sunlight.
